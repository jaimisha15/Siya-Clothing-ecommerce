How to Choose the Perfect
Hoodie for Your Body Type

The hoodie is arguably the single most important piece of clothing in modern casual dressing. Get it right and it transforms every outfit it touches. Get it wrong and you'll wear it twice before it ends up at the back of your wardrobe. Here's the complete guide to buying right the first time.

The Three Variables That Determine Fit

Before discussing body types, understand that there are three fundamental variables in any hoodie: silhouette (how loose or fitted the cut is), length (how far it drops below the waist), and fabric weight (measured in GSM ♡€” grams per square metre). These interact differently on different body types, which is why a one-size-fits-all recommendation doesn't work.

For Athletic / Muscular Builds

Your challenge is accommodation ♡€” particularly through the shoulders and chest. Standard-sized hoodies will often be too tight across the chest but too baggy through the waist. Look for:

  • Slightly oversized fit or a brand with generous cuts ♡€” avoid slim-fit entirely
  • Raglan sleeves to accommodate shoulder width without sizing up the entire body
  • A medium-length drop (hip-length) to balance the broader upper body
  • Heavy fabric (400♡€“500GSM) which drapes rather than clings

Our Noir Oversized Hoodie is specifically cut to accommodate muscular builds without sacrificing silhouette.

For Slim / Lean Builds

The current streetwear trend of genuine oversized proportions actually works well for slimmer builds ♡€” the volume adds visual mass and creates a more substantial silhouette. Consider:

  • True oversized fits with a dropped shoulder seam ♡€” these add visual width
  • Heavyweight fabrics (350GSM+) which hold shape and add bulk
  • Longer-length cuts that emphasise height and elongate the silhouette
  • Contrasting pieces below ♡€” slightly slimmer trousers balance the proportions

For Larger / Broader Builds

Proportion and clean silhouette are paramount. Avoid anything that bunches or creates excess fabric around the midsection. Key considerations:

  • A looser fit through the body but a structured shoulder seam is essential ♡€” it creates order
  • Dark, tonal colourways ♡€” navy, charcoal, black ♡€” are the most streamlining
  • Avoid thick ribbed hems or kangaroo pockets that add visual weight to the midsection
  • Long-line cuts that extend past the hip help elongate the overall silhouette

The Fabric Weight Guide

This is the most overlooked factor in hoodie selection, and arguably the most important one.

200♡€“280GSM: Lightweight

Good for layering under heavier outerwear in cooler weather, or as a standalone in hot climates. Tends to look thin and loses shape over time. Appropriate for everyday basics at lower price points.

320♡€“400GSM: Mid-Weight

The sweet spot for most year-round use. Substantial enough to hold its structure, light enough for three-season wear. This is where most premium brands operate.

400♡€“600GSM: Heavyweight

The category dominating 2026. These hoodies look and feel like investment pieces ♡€” they drape with authority, resist pilling, and maintain their shape wash after wash. Best for standalone wearing in cooler temperatures. Our heaviest offerings sit in the 500GSM range and are noticeably different in hand from anything lighter.

Silhouette Glossary

Understanding the terminology helps you shop more precisely:

  • Slim fit: follows the body contour ♡€” best for gym-lean builds who want a streamlined look
  • Regular fit: straight body, modest ease through the chest and waist ♡€” the most forgiving universal fit
  • Relaxed fit: noticeably roomier through the body ♡€” the contemporary sweet spot for casual wear
  • Oversized: dropped shoulders, wide body ♡€” intentionally generous proportions designed as a style statement
  • Boxy: shortened length, square cut ♡€” a specific silhouette that works best for taller frames

The Hoodie Buying Checklist

Before any purchase, run through these five questions:

  1. Does the shoulder seam sit exactly where I want it ♡€” at the shoulder point or deliberately dropped?
  2. Is the fabric weight appropriate for how and where I'll wear it?
  3. Does the colour work with at least three pieces I already own?
  4. Is the hood structured or limp? (Structured hoods always look more intentional)
  5. Would I pay this price again if I had to buy it three years from now?
"The right hoodie is the closest thing menswear has to a universal garment. Get the weight and proportion right, and it works with everything from tracksuit bottoms to tailored trousers."
